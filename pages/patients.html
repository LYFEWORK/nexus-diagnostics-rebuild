What to Expect from Your In-Home EEG

Your doctor has ordered an EEG study, and Nexus Diagnostics will bring the test to you. Here’s everything you need to know about what happens next — step by step.

Your EEG Journey

Step by Step, from Referral to Results

After Seeing Your Doctor

Once Nexus receives your referral and paperwork from your physician, we will contact the insurance company to ensure an authorization is not required. If authorization is needed, it may delay scheduling by 7–14 days. You do not need to do anything during this step.

Expect a Phone Call

Once approved, your local EEG Technician will contact you directly. They will go over all the details of your test, explain how to prepare beforehand, and answer any questions you have about what to expect.

Day of Your Appointment

Before the technician arrives, please:

  • Wash your hair to remove any hairspray or styling products
  • Make sure pets are in a secure area so they won’t interfere with setup
  • If possible, wear a button-up or zip-up shirt to avoid pulling off any of the leads

The technician will handle everything else — electrode placement, equipment setup, and a walkthrough of how the system works.

During the Study

Stay in view of the camera at all times. If a seizure or event occurs, it’s important for your doctor to see the episode on video.

Keep a patient diary. If a push-button event is triggered, note what symptoms you’re feeling at the time — the more detail you provide, the better your doctor can interpret the results.

Reviewing Your Results

Once the study is complete, the technician returns to remove the leads and equipment. Your study data is sent to your doctor’s office for review. After interpreting the results, your doctor will review them with you directly.

Preparation

How to Prepare for Your In-Home EEG

Wash your hair — remove hairspray, gel, and styling products
Secure your pets — keep them in a separate room during setup
Wear comfortable clothing — button-up or zip-up shirts are ideal
Clear a space — the technician will need a table or surface for equipment
Have your medications list ready — bring a list of current medications and dosages
Charge your phone — in case we need to reach you during the study

What to Bring / Have Available

Patient diary

You’ll log symptoms and events during the study

Button-up shirt

Makes electrode placement and removal easier

Your insurance card

The technician may need to verify information

A comfortable chair

You’ll want to sit in view of the camera

During Your Study

Important Reminders While Your EEG is Recording

Stay in view of the camera

This is critical. If a seizure occurs, your doctor needs video evidence to make an accurate diagnosis.

Keep your patient diary

When you press the event button, write down exactly what you’re feeling — tingling, dizziness, confusion, visual changes. Detail matters.

Continue your normal routine

Go about your day as normally as possible. The more natural your activity, the more useful the data.

Don’t disconnect anything

If a lead comes loose or you have a concern, contact us directly. Do not attempt to reattach or adjust the equipment yourself.
